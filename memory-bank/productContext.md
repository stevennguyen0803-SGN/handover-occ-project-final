# Product Context - OCC Handover System

## Problem Being Solved
Operations Control Centre handovers are currently spread across spreadsheets, email threads, and verbal updates. That creates inconsistent structure, missing follow-up ownership, weak traceability, and a high risk of open issues being lost between shifts.

## Before This System
- Shift handover information is recorded in ad hoc formats
- Open operational issues can be missed or duplicated
- There is limited auditability for who changed what and when
- High-priority issues may not have clear ownership or acknowledgment

## After This System
- Every handover is created in a standard digital format
- Open and monitoring items remain visible and can be carried to the next shift
- High and critical items are highlighted, assigned, and tracked
- Incoming shifts acknowledge important handovers in-system
- Supervisors and management can review a full audit trail

## User Journey
1. OCC staff creates a handover for a date and shift.
2. The user enters category items such as aircraft, airport, crew, weather, system, and abnormal events.
3. The system validates business rules, generates the reference ID, and stores the record.
4. Incoming shift reviews the handover, acknowledges if required, and continues work on open items.
5. Open or monitoring items are carried forward into the next shift handover.
6. Supervisors and management review dashboard summaries, exports, and audit history.

## UX Goals
- Simplicity: a shift handover can be created without training-heavy workflows
- Speed: entering and reviewing urgent operational issues should be fast
- Reliability: data must persist, validate correctly, and retain change history
- Visibility: open, monitoring, high, and critical items must stand out

## Important Business Rules
- Mandatory handover fields are required on every record.
- Reference IDs are generated by the system and are never user-editable.
- Open and monitoring items remain visible and must be eligible for carry-forward.
- High and critical open items require ownership.
- Resolved items become immutable except for remarks.
- All mutations must write to the audit log.
- High and critical handovers require acknowledgment by the incoming shift.
- Soft delete applies to handovers and items; hard delete is not allowed.
